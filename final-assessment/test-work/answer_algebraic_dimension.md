# Algebraic Dimension of a Complex Torus Containing an Abelian Variety

## Question
Let $X$ be a complex torus of dimension $17$ that contains an abelian variety of dimension $10$. What is the smallest possible value of the algebraic dimension $a(X)$?

## Answer

The smallest possible algebraic dimension is **10**.

## Explanation

### Step 1: Understanding the definitions

- **Complex torus**: $X = \mathbb{C}^{17}/\Lambda$ where $\Lambda$ is a lattice of rank $34$
- **Algebraic dimension** $a(X)$: Transcendence degree of the field of meromorphic functions on $X$ over $\mathbb{C}$
- For a complex torus, $0 \le a(X) \le \dim X = 17$
- $a(X) = \dim X$ if and only if $X$ is an abelian variety (projective)

### Step 2: Key theorem

For a complex torus $T$, the algebraic dimension equals the maximum dimension of an abelian subvariety of $T$:

$$a(T) = \max\{ \dim S : S \subset T \text{ is an abelian subvariety}\}$$

**Reason**: Meromorphic functions on a torus come from abelian subvarieties via Fourier series. The field of meromorphic functions is generated by functions pulled back from abelian quotients.

### Step 3: Applying to our case

We are given:
- $X$ contains an abelian variety $A$ of dimension $10$
- Therefore, $\dim A = 10 \le a(X)$ by the theorem

Thus:
$$a(X) \ge 10$$

### Step 4: Showing 10 is attainable

Construct $X$ as a product:
$$X = A \times B$$

where:
- $A$ is an abelian variety of dimension $10$ (so $a(A)=10$)
- $B$ is a **non-algebraic** complex torus of dimension $7$ (so $a(B)=0$)

Then:
- $\dim X = 10 + 7 = 17$
- $A \subset X$ as a subtorus
- $a(X) = a(A) + a(B) = 10 + 0 = 10$

Such a $B$ exists — take a general complex torus of dimension $7$ with no non-constant meromorphic functions (e.g., a torus with generic period matrix).

### Step 5: Why not smaller than 10?

If $a(X) < 10$, then by the theorem, **every** abelian subvariety of $X$ would have dimension $\le a(X) < 10$. But we are explicitly given that $X$ contains an abelian variety of dimension $10$, which would then have dimension $\le a(X) < 10$, a contradiction.

Therefore:
$$a(X) \ge 10$$

Since $a(X) = 10$ is achievable, the minimum is $10$.

## Final Answer

$$\boxed{10}$$
